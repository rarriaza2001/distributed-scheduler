Commands are instructions / intended actions

SchedulerCommand =
  | DispatchJob
  | MarkJobRunning
  | MarkJobSucceeded
  | MarkJobFailed
  | RequeueJob
  | DeclareWorkerDead
  | SweepStuckJobs

They model the scheduler’s control-plane decisions. They clarify:
1. what authority the scheduler has
2. what state transitions are legitimate
3. what internal operations exist

Who writes them: scheduler

Who reads them
- internal scheduler logic
- logs / audit

DispatchJob

Scheduler decision:
- assign this job to this worker
- give it lease until X

MarkJobRunning
Represents:
- worker has actually started execution
- Useful because dispatch and actual start are not always the same moment.

MarkJobSucceeded
Represents final success transition.

MarkJobFailed
Represents failure outcome and error metadata.
- does not necessarily mean terminal
- may later lead to retry or dead-letter

RequeueJob

Scheduler says:
- put this job back into eligible future execution at nextRetryAt
- Used for:
    - retry
    - worker timeout recovery
    - stuck-job recovery

DeclareWorkerDead

Scheduler conclusion:
- this worker timed out / is no longer trusted alive
- Used to trigger:
    - job recovery
    - capacity removal
    - observability

SweepStuckJobs
A control action representing:
- perform scan for timed-out or orphaned jobs
- This is more of an operational command than a job-level command.
