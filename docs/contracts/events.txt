Events are facts that happened.

SchedulerEvent =
  | JobDispatched
  | JobStarted
  | JobSucceeded
  | JobFailed
  | JobRequeued
  | WorkerRegistered
  | WorkerHeartbeatReceived
  | WorkerTimedOut
  | JobMovedToDeadLetter

Events are useful for:
- audit trail
- monitoring
- debugging
- metrics derivation
- incident analysis

Commands say:
- do this

Events say:
- this happened

Who writes them
- scheduler
- sometimes worker-originated facts become normalized into events by scheduler

Who reads them
- audit
- metrics
- logs
- dashboards
- postmortems

Event by event

JobDispatched: scheduler assigned job to worker

JobStarted: worker actually began execution

JobSucceeded: execution completed successfully

JobFailed: execution failed

JobRequeued: scheduler made job eligible again for future execution

WorkerRegistered: worker joined system

WorkerHeartbeatReceived: liveness signal received

WorkerTimedOut: worker considered dead or unavailable after timeout window

JobMovedToDeadLetter: retries exhausted or terminal failure reached
